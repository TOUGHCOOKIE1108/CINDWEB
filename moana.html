MOANA

  • The relationship between how far I'll go and Moana

    • The song reflects Moana's internal struggle between her longing for the sea and her family's expectaions, driving her determination to embark on her journey. It portrays her pursuit of self-discovery, courage, dreams, and connection to nature, expressing her emotional growth.

  • Introduction of the music

    • "How Far I'll Go" based in F major, the bridge transions to D minor, highlighting inner turmoil and uncertainty, mirroring Moana's inner conflict. The tempo starts slow, quickens in the chorus with drums and strings adding tension and dynamism. Natural sound effects like ocean waves enhance the theme. The latter part includes choral harmonies, enriching emotional depth and reflecting Moana's cultural background and the oceanic spirit of the film. Some melodies and rhythms draw from Polynesian traditional music, using irregular beats to mimic Pacific Island styles.
